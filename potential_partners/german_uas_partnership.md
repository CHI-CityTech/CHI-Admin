## Executive Synthesis
The DAAD/UAS networking event frames a partnership ecosystem where German Universities of Applied Sciences (UAS) are positioned as **high-value applied-learning partners** for U.S. institutions seeking workforce relevance, work-integrated learning, and scalable international collaboration. Speakers emphasized a network-based partnership model (using multipliers like DAAD, liaison offices, chambers, consulates) and offered operational guidance: successful partnerships must include registrars/advising/career services early to avoid failures in credit recognition and program mapping.  

**Strategic move for CHI:** align this supply chain of partners with an anchoring European hub at **Modul University Vienna**, using **Tourism + UNESCO** as the primary applied research bridge.

## Event Summary (CHI lens)

### A. What German UAS institutions bring
UAS institutions emphasized:
- practice-based teaching and **transfer** into real-world contexts
- deep regional anchoring and ties to industry and societal actors
- strong offerings in STEM + business + design, with expanding research capacity
- practical internship / dual-study models valued by U.S. partners  

### B. Networking architecture: multipliers over one-offs
The UAS7 talk explicitly argues for scaling transatlantic collaboration using networks:
- DAAD, consulates, liaison offices, chambers of commerce
- German Centers for Research & Innovation (DWI)
- collaborative coalition-building as a cultural norm in this space 

### C. Partnership success depends on *operations*, not only leadership
ACRAO stressed that partnerships often start at leadership level but fail without operational stakeholders:
- registrars / transfer evaluation
- advising
- career services and student affairs
- clear mapping of credits, grade scales, transcript documentation, and policies  

### D. Business/startup pathway multiplier (highly actionable)
German-American Chamber of Commerce offered:
- access to **850+ member companies** for internships
- **J-1 visa sponsorship** for interns
- university **spin-off bootcamp + pitch night** programs 

### E. Delegation signals (UAS goals)
Delegates openly sought:
- new U.S. partners
- summer schools / visiting professorships
- sustainability partnerships
- applied research + industry integration
- dual diploma programs described as a strong current demand in the SUNY system
